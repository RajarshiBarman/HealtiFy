Japanese Encephalitis

This is a viral infection primarily transmitted through mosquitoes, particularly in rural and agricultural areas of Asia. The causative agent, the Japanese encephalitis virus (JEV), can lead to severe inflammation of the brain. Symptoms range from mild flu-like manifestations to more severe conditions, including high fever, headaches, neck stiffness, and, in extreme cases, neurological complications.
